T1 Ch2 — Problem Solving
Practice Exam  |  Name:    |  Date:  
Learning Links Pre-Knowledge
  1. What is a "problem" in everyday life?
  2. Name 2 ways people might try to solve problems.
  3. What do you think "insight" means?
  4. Can past experience ever make solving a problem harder? How?
Critical + Creative Thinking Questions
  1. Think of a time you were stuck on something. What made it hard?
  2. Why might someone else solve the same problem faster than you?
  3. Have you ever had a sudden "aha" moment? What was it like?
  4. When could thinking creatively be better than following steps?
Practice Questions Understanding & Fluency
  1. Define a problem.
  2. Difference between well-defined and ill-defined problems.
  3. What is problem representation?
  4. What is insight?
  5. Define fixation.
  1. What is functional fixedness?
  2. What is a mental set?
  3. Explain means-end analysis.
  4. What is trial and error?
  5. Define divergent vs convergent thinking.
Practice Questions Reasoning & Application
  1. Give one example of a well-defined and one ill-defined problem.
  2. Why can fixation make problems harder to solve?
  3. How does changing how you represent a problem help solve it?
  4. Why are insight solutions hard to predict?
  5. Explain how means-end analysis could help with studying for exams.
  6. Why might an expert solve a problem faster than a beginner?
Enrichment Optional — but interesting

Challenge yourself — these questions have no single right answer.

  1. How do AI systems (like chatbots) use problem-solving strategies similar to humans?
  2. Do you think creativity is just "divergent thinking," or something more? Explain.
  3. Design a simple experiment to test insight (e.g., giving people puzzles).
